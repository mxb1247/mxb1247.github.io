Camden Market

Camden Market is defiantly a must do when you are visiting London. Take a Sunday afternoon, put on your walking shoes, and make your way down to Camden Town where the Camden Market is located. The market offers a large variety of restaurants, shopping, and bars and depending on what you are in the mood for you will always be able to find it in the Camden market and have fun doing it too.

London Eye

The London Eye has always been an iconic landmark that was on my bucket list for years. As you walk you up to the London eye you see a breathtaking structure that is so high up that it hurts your neck to look. Even though going on the London Eye means standing in long lines and pay a steep price to go on it, once you go on it and make it to the top you see a sight like no other. You are able to see miles of London. This landmark is deffently a must do and something you can't miss out.

Stonehenge

As I saw the stones from the distance, I started getting chills. The place was magical and the view was breathtaking. When I finally arrived at the top of the mountain I could appreciate how the stones were perfectly placed in a circle which makes you wonder about the mystery of Stonehenge and its creation. The feeling of grandiosity and excitement that this site gives you is incredible. Stonehenge is a must do when you are in London, it is defiantly a once in a lifetime opportunity.

Wicked at the London Theater

Growing up with the movie of the Wizard of Ozz and that being your costume every year in elementary school, you can imagine going to Wicked was definitely a must do when I visited London. Even though the tickets were not cheap, seeing Wicked was definitely a life-changing experience. The actors in the play were unbelievable and even though you have seen Wicked in New York City you sill need to see Wicked, London style.
